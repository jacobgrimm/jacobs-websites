Overnight Layover Guide

One Night
in CDMX

You've got a window between landing and your 6am departure. Here's how to spend it in three of the city's best neighborhoods — no hotel, no wasted time.

Start ~10:00 PM
Return to Airport ~3:00 AM
Neighborhoods Centro · Roma · Condesa · Juárez
Transport Uber only
⚠ Safety basics before you go:
  • Always use Uber — never hail a street taxi
  • Stick to well-lit, busy streets and trust your gut
  • Keep your phone in a pocket, not in your hand on the street
  • The metro shuts down around midnight — you're on Uber after that
  • Cards accepted at most bars; carry some pesos for street food
10:00 PM
↑ Uber from airport
Centro Histórico
El Zócalo
Landmark

One of the largest city squares in the world, and at night it's genuinely spectacular. The Metropolitan Cathedral and National Palace glow across the plaza. There are always people, performers, and vendors. Start here — it puts the whole city in context immediately.

Duration: 30–45 min. Just walk around and absorb it — you don't need to go inside anything. Grab a street taco from a vendor while you're here.
10:45 PM
↑ 3 blocks walk
Centro Histórico
Calle Regina
Bar Street

A pedestrian-only street a few blocks south of the Zócalo, lined with local bars, live music spilling out of doorways, and people hanging out on plastic chairs. This is where locals drink, not tourists — cheap mezcal, cold beer, zero pretension. Great way to ease into the night.

Duration: ~45 min. Pick a bar with outdoor seating, have 1–2 drinks, and watch the street do its thing.
11:45 PM
↑ Uber to Roma Norte
Roma Norte
Licorerìa Limantour
Cocktail Bar

Consistently ranked among the best cocktail bars in Latin America. On Álvaro Obregón in the heart of Roma Norte — a beautiful tree-lined boulevard that's lively at night. The drinks are creative and mezcal-forward. Cozy inside, tables spill onto the sidewalk. This is peak CDMX bar culture.

Order: Mango y Chili, Banana Daiquiri, or ask what's seasonal. Open until 2am Thu–Sat. Arriving before midnight gives you a good window before last call.
1:00 AM
↑ Walk south to Condesa
Condesa
Parque México
Night Walk

Walk south from Roma into Condesa and pass through this gorgeous Art Deco park — open 24 hours. Fountains, stone paths, huge old trees. The streets surrounding it are lined with bars still buzzing at this hour. Pick up a late-night taco from one of the street vendors posted up outside.

Duration: 30–45 min stroll. Explore Ámsterdam Ave that circles around the park — some of the prettiest urban walking in the city.
1:45 AM
↑ Uber to Juárez
Juárez / Zona Rosa
Handshake Speakeasy
Top 50 World

End the night at one of the top cocktail bars on the planet — ranked in the World's 50 Best Bars list. Hidden entrance, dark and intimate interior, cocktails that are genuinely unlike anything you've had. The drinks are creative to the point of theatrical: tableside flames, unexpected ingredients, perfect balance. Worth every peso.

Book ahead at handshake.bar — reservations fill 2 weeks out, open until 2am on weekends. This is tight with a 10pm arrival so book the last slot available. If fully booked, the Zona Rosa streets nearby have plenty of late-night alternatives that don't close until 3–4am.
~3:00 AM
↑ Uber to airport
Aeropuerto Internacional
AICM — Terminal
Back to Reality

Uber directly to the airport. From Juárez it's 25–35 minutes depending on traffic. Getting there by 3–3:30am gives you plenty of cushion before a 6am flight.

Note: AICM has two terminals (T1 and T2) — confirm yours in advance. The Uber drop-off and check-in process can be slow at odd hours so don't cut it too close.
// Practical Notes
🚗
Always use Uber
Same app, works great in CDMX. Much safer than hailing street taxis. Download before you land and make sure your payment method is set.
💸
Money
Cards work at all the bars listed. Carry 200–400 pesos cash (~$10–20 USD) for street food, tacos, and vendors. ATMs are fine in Roma/Condesa.
🍹
What to drink
You're in Mexico — order mezcal. Or a mezcal-based cocktail. Tequila is fine but mezcal is the move here. Modelo pairs with everything.
📍
Neighborhoods
Roma Norte and Condesa are the safest and most walkable. Stay on main streets at night and you'll be totally fine. Trust your instincts.
🕐
CDMX runs late
The city doesn't get going until 9–10pm. Don't be surprised if Limantour is quiet at 11 but packed by midnight. This works in your favor.
✈️
Airport buffer
Leave wherever you are by 2:30–3am to be safe. AICM can move slowly at weird hours. Confirm T1 vs T2 before you go.
